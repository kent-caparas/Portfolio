An isometric 3D room you walked through to find my work. Objects on the desk
were projects. The bookshelf was the blog. It was the most fun I've ever had
building a site and the worst site I've ever built — people loaded it, said
"whoa," and then couldn't figure out how to read a single post.

Archived rather than deleted, because the lesson was expensive and I'd like to
remember it: a portfolio's job is to get out of the way of the work. The current
site is the correction.
